About Me

I am a multidisciplinary computational biologist and Senior Research Scientist working at the intersection of bioinformatics, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and biomedical sciences.

My research focuses on developing computational frameworks to analyze complex biological datasets, including single-cell RNA sequencing, transcriptomics, next-generation sequencing, and multi-omics data.

My goal is to transform large-scale biological data into meaningful scientific insights that support disease understanding, biomarker discovery, and precision medicine.

Featured Project

RNAseqMVA: Machine Learning Framework for RNA-seq Analysis

RNAseqMVA is an R-based computational framework developed to evaluate supervised machine-learning approaches for RNA-seq transcriptomic classification.

Methods

  • Support Vector Machine (SVM)
  • Random Forest
  • K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN)
  • Transcriptomic feature analysis
  • Reproducible computational workflows

The project demonstrated how artificial intelligence approaches can identify molecular signatures and improve classification of biological samples.
